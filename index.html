Transportation

Public transport

Traveling is Israel is pretty straight-forward: you have options of railways, buses, taxi or on your own vehicle. Visit the website of Israel railways, pick your preferred language (it supports Hebrew, Arabic, English and Russian) then choose your destination station, the origin station and either time of arrival or departure. You will be presented with a timetable, suitable train numbers.

There are couple of application to help you out with understanding your routes:

Google Maps is a viable option to draw out reasonable ways of reaching your endpoint: different bus routes and rough timetable Rav-kav. Rav-kav is a plastic card to pay for your trips on train or bus and an app to load your preferrable ticket and Want to use the features of Rav-kav but can't be bothered to handle another plactic card in your wallet? Moovit application provides exactly that: simply link your credit card to an app and scan qr-codes as an alternative payment method.

Private transport

If you already had driving licence upon your arrival to Israel you can drive your car using it for a year since date of arrival.

After a year has passed there are two ways forward:

  1. If you have driving licence for less than five years you'll have to pass driving test to get israeli Driving licence.
  2. If you have driving licence for more than five years you'll just need to visit Ministry of transport, apply for israeli driving licence and pay your fee. It will be waiting for you in postal office after being made. While it isn't made yet, you may you temporary licence which will be available for you to print from your personal area at gov.il website.

Hebrew

Living in Israel without knowing Hebrew is an achiveable goal, but it also involves lots of complications in your daily activities: don't you want to avoid additional struggles on your journey to improvement? At the beginning you might look into default solution to studying new language - Duolingo. But it has it's limitations. Ulpan system is here to help you out: learn Hebrew with other Olims. Ulpan aleph will provide you with intense introduction to Hebrew. Every weekday you will learn something new about the logic, structure and rules of Hebrew. Ask your aliyah assistant about further information regarding sign up for ulpan.
