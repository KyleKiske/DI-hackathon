Upon arrival in airport your will be presented with a choice of your desired "Kupat Holim" - mandatory government healthcare companies. Your will pick between "Clalit", "Maccabi", "Meuhedet" and "leumit". Choose your desired one based on reviews and availability of any in the vicinity of your place of living.

There are lots of government systems you have to engage with to start your life in Israel: Ministry of Internal Affairs, Ministry of Transport, Ministry of Immigration etc.

Traveling is Israel is pretty straight-forward: you have options of railways, buses, taxi or on your own vehicle. Visit the website of Israel railways, pick your preferred language (it supports Hebrew, Arabic, English and Russian) then choose your destination station, the origin station and either time of arrival or departure. You will be presented with a timetable and suitable train numbers.

There are couple of application to help you out with understanding your routes:

  • Google Maps is a viable option to draw out reasonable ways of reaching your endpoint: different bus routes and rough timetable
  • Rav-kav. Rav-kav is a plastic card to pay for your trips on train or bus and an app to load your preferrable ticket and
  • Want to use the features of Rav-kav but can't be bothered to handle another plactic card in your wallet? Moovit application provides exactly that: simply link your credit card to an app and scan qr-codes as an alternative payment method.
